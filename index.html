About Me

Hi! I'm Max Haberer, and I'm a high school student in Texas!

I just wrapped up the 2024 Bell AVR competition season, placing 4th at the national championship as a member of team Zephyrus! I was the lead software developer, electrical, and mechanical engineer for the AVR drone this year. I used Python, C++, PX4, MavLink, and QGroundControl to automate drone movement and task execution, including transporting cargo with an imbedded electromagnet and scanning areas of thermal activity. Some highlights of the season include controlling an electromagnet using a switch on the AVR's remote controller, and earning a Judge's Choice award for our autonomous flight capabilities. I've learned a lot from this experience and I look forward to educating younger members of the club, preparing them to tackle the challenges of next year without me or the other seniors.

I'm working on a little passion project at the moment, simple_ytdl, a lightweight & easy-to-use program for downloading Youtube videos and audio at high quality! Feel free to check it out!

I'm currently applying to college! I hope to major in Electrical & Computer Engineering, so that I can focus on my interests in robotics and programming.
